Ian Murdock

Ian Murdock is the founder of Debian, one of the first Linux distributions and still one of the most popular with millions of users worldwide. He led the Debian project from its inception in 1993 to 1996, building Debian from an idea to a worldwide organization of more than 100 people in less than three years. Today, Debian is widely considered one of the most successful and influential open source projects in the world. Ian is also co-founder and chairman of Progeny Linux Systems, a provider of Linux-based software and services based in Indianapolis, Indiana. A longtime Linux user, developer, and advocate, Ian was the original architect of dpkg, the first package management system for Linux. He was also a founding director of Linux International, Software in the Public Interest, and the Open Source Initiative. Ian holds a B.S. in computer science from Purdue University.
