Michael Hart

Michael S. Hart was one of the first 100 members of the virtual community that has since become known as the Internet and World Wide Web. In 1971 with the great help of two operators of what was then a huge million dollar mainframe computer that is today totally outclassed by calculators well under $100, started what turned out to be the first "site" on what became the Internet--but no one really paid much attention to this site until 1988 when the Internet finally passed 250,000 members and it finally had a large enough population to support conversations on world wide ranges of topics. By 1991 the word "Internet" finally was able to appear for the first time on the front page or cover of any of the major media, and fittingly enough it was the article about that very Project Gutenberg site in the Wall St. Journal.

Michael received a degree in "Human-Machine Interfaces" [1973], but academia was not interested in the distribution of "virtual libraries of free electronic texts" so after three aborted grad school efforts to pursue the "Neo-Industrial Revolution," he is still pursuing this effort as the largest "shoestring project." At this moment Project Gutenberg is approaching 5,000 "eTexts." Michael hopes to post 10,000 eTexts in his lifetime without any major funding, and hopes to post 1,000,000 with major funding.
